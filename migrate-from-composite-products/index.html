Switching guide

Moving from Composite Products?
We will make it painless.

Composite Products is capable, and at about £114 a year it should be. If what you actually need is configurable kits with per-item personalisation, BundleBoss does that in one plugin for less. Here is how the move works, and the honest state of it.

Why shops switch

One plugin, not three

To get configurable kits plus personalisation from the official extensions you stack Composite Products, Product Bundles and Product Add-Ons. BundleBoss is one tool, one settings screen, one thing to update.

Personalisation built in

On Pro, add a name, number or logo upload to each item in the kit, and a different one per unit. The piece the official extensions are not built to do even side by side.

Less to pay

BundleBoss Solo is £79 a year against about £114 for Composite Products alone, before you add the others. See the full comparison.

How your setup maps across

Most Composite Products ideas have a direct home in BundleBoss. The names change, the thinking does not.

Two ways to move, both keep your originals

Whichever you pick, your Composite products are never touched. You end up with BundleBoss bundles as drafts: review them, change anything, and publish only when you are happy.

Do it yourself, in one click

On Pro, the Tools screen spots your Composite products and converts each into a draft bundle. Components become choice slots with their pools, quantities, optional flags and discounts. Anything it cannot map, it lists per product, so nothing disappears quietly.

Or hand it to us

Rather not touch it yourself? In WooCommerce, Products, Export gives you a file of your products. Send it over with a line on which ones matter most, and we build the drafts for you. Seeing them costs nothing.
