Product options

Sell choices,
not a wall of variations.

A hoodie in twelve colours and seven sizes is 84 variations if you build it the WooCommerce way: 84 records to price, stock and keep straight, for one product. BundleBoss lets the product stay simple and adds the choices on top. Shoppers see swatches and size buttons exactly as they would on a variable product; your admin sees one product with one stock line.

Define the choices once, use them everywhere

An option set is a list you write one time: your colours, your sizes, your bag weights. Attach a set to any simple product and the choices appear on its page. Change the set later and every product using it follows. There is a shelf of ready-made starters, so a clothing store is a couple of clicks from a full colour and size range.

Charge for the choices that cost you more

Any option can carry its own charge, shown right on the button: 4XL adds £2, a printed label adds £1, the heavier bag costs its share more. Overrides per product cover the awkward cases, and the charge follows the shopper's pick into the total, the cart, the order and the confirmation email.

It works inside bundles too

This is the part a separate options plugin cannot give you. Because options and bundles are one system, every bundle that includes the product inherits its choices: a fixed-price pack of three hoodies where every hoodie picks its own colour and size, a build-a-box where each product offers its choices before it goes in the box. One engine, one total, one cart line.

Included, not another plugin

Shops usually end up buying a bundles plugin, an options plugin and a swatches plugin, then hoping the three agree about prices in the cart. Product options are part of BundleBoss Pro: same admin, same engine that already handles your bundle maths, nothing extra to buy or keep compatible. And where you genuinely need per-version stock, normal WooCommerce variations still work alongside, including inside bundles.
