## The Bundle Promotions tab

Each row is one feature: a switch, a one-line summary of its current state, and an **Edit** button for its settings. A feature that is off stays visible as one calm line, so you always know what is available.

</figure>

## Sale countdown timer

The sale's one home. **Edit** holds the **sale price** and its **start and end dates**; while the window runs, the bundle charges the sale price and the product page shows a live **"Sale ending in"** countdown with a depleting bar. When the window closes, the price and the page go back to normal on their own. Quantity tiers stack on the sale price, so "on sale AND cheaper by the case" works.

## You might also like

Hand-pick other bundles to show in a **carousel** below this one. A simple way to move shoppers between related pages, from a starter pack to a premium one, or between seasonal kits.

## The rest of the rows

- **Share this build**: a button that gives the shopper a link reopening their exact picks. Per-bundle opt-in; see [shareable build links](/docs/share-links/). <span class="pro-pill">Pro</span>
- **Personalisation for the whole order**: fields asked once for the entire bundle, a shared logo or a delivery note; see [personalisation](/docs/personalisation/). <span class="pro-pill">Pro</span>
- **Gift options**: recipient, message, optional wrap and a gift receipt; see [gift mode](/docs/gift-mode/). <span class="pro-pill">Pro</span>
- **Buy X, get Y**: the cheapest items go free or discounted at a quantity; see [Buy X Get Y](/docs/bogo/). <span class="pro-pill">Pro</span>
- **Frequently bought together**: companion offers that live on simple product pages; see [FBT](/docs/fbt/). <span class="pro-pill">Pro</span>

[Quantity discount tiers](/docs/pricing/) stay on the Bundle Pricing tab, because they are pricing rather than a promotion: "buy 2 or more bundles, save 15%".

<div class="callout tip"><span class="callout-label">Tip</span><p>Pair the sale countdown with a quantity tier so the offer rewards buying more, and the carousel to point shoppers at the next bundle once they have committed to one.</p></div>
