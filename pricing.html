Fair questions

Why pay when there are cheaper bundle plugins?
The cheap ones do fixed bundles. So does our free plugin. Pro is for the Builder: configurators with rules that stop customers buying combinations that do not work. The only comparable product costs $149 a year and has not shipped a meaningful feature in years.

How does this compare to the official extensions?
To get both halves of what BundleBoss does, WooCommerce.com charges for two separate products: Product Bundles plus Composite Products, over $200 a year together. BundleBoss Solo gives you both halves, designed to work as one, for $99.

What happens if I cancel?
The plugin keeps working, your bundles stay intact, you stop receiving updates and support. Your data is never held hostage.

Is the free version crippled?
No. It does what the official $79-a-year fixed-bundle extension does, free, forever. Pro adds the Builder, not the basics.
