Why it normally takes three plugins

If you have tried to let customers build their own bundle, you have hit the wall. Product Bundles locks every slot, so it cannot do a different size or colour per unit. So you add Composite Products for the configuring, then Product Add-Ons for the names and logos. Three plugins, three settings screens, three things to keep updated, and a checkout that still cannot quite do what the customer asked.

BundleBoss is one plugin that does all of it. A club orders 20 training tops: 6 navy medium, 5 black large, the rest a mix, a player name on every back. One product, one configurator, one line at checkout, one order to fulfil. That is the thing the three-plugin stack cannot do, even side by side.

The three you would buy

To match what BundleBoss Pro does using official WooCommerce.com extensions, you would reach for Product Bundles (about £60 a year), Composite Products (about £112) for the configurable, choice-based building, and Product Add-Ons (about £49) for personalisation. That is roughly £220 a year, across three separate plugins. BundleBoss Solo is £79 and built as one.

Questions people ask before switching

Can I bring my Composite Products bundles across?

Yes. Pro includes a one-click importer that reads your Composite Products setups and rebuilds them as BundleBoss bundles, and there is a step-by-step migration guide.

Is it really one plugin, not a stack of add-ons?

Yes. Configuring, choice slots and per-item personalisation are all in BundleBoss. No separate Composite or Product Add-Ons licence, and no plugins to make talk to each other.

Is the free version enough, or do I need Pro?

The free build already does configurable, per-unit bundles, including variable products with sizes and colours, plus pricing, stock and fulfilment pick lists. Pro adds the Builder: choice slots, personalisation, the full-page configurator and more.

What happens to my existing variable products?

They drop straight in as bundle items. Each keeps its sizes and colours, and the customer configures every unit on the bundle page.

Is there anything Composite does that BundleBoss does not?

Composite has a few very advanced per-component pricing scenarios. For everyday configurable bundles, kits and personalised runs, BundleBoss does the job in one plugin and adds the per-unit configuration and personalisation Composite cannot. The table above shows where each fits.
