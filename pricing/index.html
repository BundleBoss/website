Common questions

Why pay when there are cheaper bundle plugins?

The cheap ones do fixed bundles. So does our free plugin. Pro is for the Builder: configurators with rules that stop customers buying combinations that do not work. Very few plugins go that far, and the ones that do sell it as a separate, pricier extension. BundleBoss builds it in.

How does this compare to the official extensions?

To match what BundleBoss Pro does using official WooCommerce.com extensions you would buy three: Product Bundles (£60 a year), Composite Products (£112) for build-your-own, and Product Add-Ons (£49) for personalisation. That is about £220 a year, across three plugins stitched together. BundleBoss Solo gives you all of it, designed to work as one, for £79. And it personalises every unit individually, a different name on each shirt, which the three are not built to do even side by side.

How does the free trial work?

Every Pro plan starts with a 14-day free trial that does not ask for a payment method. With the 30-day money-back guarantee on top, you get about six weeks to decide with nothing at risk.

What happens if I cancel?

The plugin keeps working, your bundles stay intact, you stop receiving updates and support. Your data is never held hostage.

What if I drop from Pro back to Free?

Nothing is lost. Everything free keeps working untouched, and your Builder configuration (choice slots, rules, layouts) is kept safe and springs back exactly as it was if you ever return.

Is the free version limited?

No. It covers everything the official fixed-bundle extension charges £60 a year for, free on wordpress.org. Pro adds the Builder, not the basics.

Will it work with my theme?

The storefront layouts measure the space your theme gives them and adapt; we test on Storefront, Astra, Kadence and OceanWP. If something looks off in yours, email us; we treat theme glitches as bugs, not edge cases.
